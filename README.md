# Chocolate-Sales-Performance-Dashboard
🍫 Excited to share my latest Power BI project — Chocolate Sales Performance Dashboard!
After working extensively with real sales data, I built a 4-page interactive dashboard that transforms raw numbers into actionable business insights. Here's a breakdown of the full project:
________________________________________
📌 Purpose of the Project
The goal was to analyze chocolate sales performance across multiple countries, products, and sales representatives — covering data from January 2023 to September 2024. The dashboard was built to help business stakeholders instantly understand where revenue is coming from, which products are driving growth, how sales teams are performing, and where operational risks like cancellations exist.
________________________________________
🛠️ Tools & Features Used
•	Microsoft Power BI Desktop — for data modeling, DAX calculations, and visual design
•	Microsoft Excel (.xlsx) — as the primary data source with 4,998 rows across 7 columns
•	DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) — to create calculated measures including Cancellation Rate, Sum of Amount, Count of Sales Persons, Count of Products, and more
•	Power BI Map Visual — bubble map showing country-wise sales distribution across UK, USA, India, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand
•	Slicers & Cross-filtering — month slicer, product filter, enabling dynamic data exploration
•	Custom Background Design — chocolate-themed background image for visual storytelling and brand alignment
•	Multiple Chart Types — Donut charts, horizontal bar charts, clustered bar charts, line charts, and KPI cards all used purposefully
________________________________________
📊 Key Takeaways from the Data
✅ Top Products: Orange Choco, Drinking Coco, and 99% Dark & Pure consistently ranked in the top 3 by amount
✅ Top Sales Person: Brien Boise — highest performer by both boxes and amount ($2.2M)
✅ Order Health: 82.43% of orders were successfully delivered, with a 4.5% cancellation rate — a risk area worth monitoring
✅ Seasonality: Sales peaked in mid-year months (June–August) with a noticeable dip in October–November, suggesting opportunities for targeted seasonal campaigns
✅ Product Breadth: 22 unique products sold through 25 sales representatives across 6 countries — showing strong market diversification
________________________________________
💡 Final Words
This dashboard is more than just charts — it's a decision-making tool. In one glance, a sales manager can identify which region needs attention, which product to promote next quarter, and which sales rep deserves recognition. The interactive slicers allow anyone — technical or non-technical — to explore the data on their own terms.
Data storytelling is a powerful skill, and projects like this reinforce why tools like Power BI are game-changers in the world of business analytics. If you're working with sales data and want to move beyond spreadsheets, I highly recommend diving into Power BI.
